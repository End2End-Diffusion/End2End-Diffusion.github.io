Overview

We tackle a fundamental question: Can we jointly tune the LDM and VAE in an end-to-end manner to best optimize the final generation performance? While it's technically straightforward to implement end-to-end LDM training by back-propagating the diffusion loss to the VAE, our experiments reveal this naive approach is ineffective. The diffusion loss encourages learning a simpler latent space structure which is easier for denoising objectives, but leads to reduced generation performance. Our method, REPA-E, overcomes this limitation by using representation alignment loss to jointly optimize both components while preventing latent space degradation.

Through extensive evaluations, we demonstrate that our end-to-end training approach offers three key advantages:

End-to-End Training Leads to Accelerated Generation Performance: REPA-E speeds up diffusion training by over 17× and 45× compared to REPA and vanilla training recipes, respectively. Using SiT-XL, REPA-E reaches an FID of 4.06 within just 400k steps, significantly outperforming REPA which only reaches a final FID of 5.9 after 4M steps.

End-to-End Training Improves VAE Latent-Space Structure: Jointly tuning the VAE and diffusion model automatically enhances the latent space structure across different architectures. For SD-VAE, REPA-E reduces high-frequency noise to learn a smoother representation. For IN-VAE with over-smoothed latents, it adds detail to better support generation performance.

End-to-End Tuning Improves VAE Performance: End-to-end tuned VAEs can be used as drop-in replacements for their original counterparts, offering accelerated generation performance across diverse architectures and training settings. Our system achieves state-of-the-art results with gFID of 1.90 without classifier-free guidance and 1.29 with guidance.

Approach

1. Motivating End-to-End Training with REPA

Training latent diffusion models typically involves a two-stage process: first training a VAE for dimensionality reduction, then training a diffusion model in the latent space. While end-to-end training would be ideal, we identify three key insights that motivate our approach:

  • Naive End-to-End Tuning is Ineffective: Backpropagating diffusion loss to the VAE creates a simpler latent space with lower spatial variance. This reduces diffusion loss but degrades generation quality by failing to model the original image distribution.
  • Higher Representation Alignment Improves Generation: Higher CKNNA scores correlate with better generation performance, suggesting representation alignment as a better objective for end-to-end training than diffusion loss.
  • VAE Features Limit Alignment: Representation alignment saturates at ~0.4 (max 1.0) due to VAE latent space bottleneck. Backpropagating alignment loss to the VAE improves CKNNA by over 27%.

2. REPA-E: Representation Alignment for End-to-End Tuning

Based on these insights, we propose REPA-E for jointly tuning both the VAE and diffusion model using representation alignment loss:

  • Batch-Norm Layer: Enables differentiable normalization between the VAE and diffusion model during training.
  • End-to-End Alignment Loss: Aligns diffusion model features with pretrained vision model, and backpropagating the alignment loss through both the diffusion model and the VAE encoder.
  • Diffusion Loss with Stop-Gradient: Prevents degradation of latent space structure by limiting diffusion loss to diffusion model parameters.
  • VAE Regularization: Maintains reconstruction performance through MSE, LPIPS, and GAN losses.

Key Outcomes of REPA-E

1. End-to-End Training Leads to Accelerated Generation Performance

  • Training efficiency improvement: REPA-E achieves gFID of 4.07 in just 80 epochs, outperforming MaskDiT (5.69 with 1600 epochs) and FasterDiT (7.91 with 400 epochs)
  • Robust performance gains: Demonstrates consistent improvements regardless of model scales (SiT-B/L/XL) or VAE architectures (SD-VAE, IN-VAE, VA-VAE)
  • Enhanced image quality across training: Using identical noise and labels, REPA-E generates structurally superior images compared to REPA baseline at 50K, 100K, and 400K training iterations

2. End-to-End Training Improves VAE Latent-Space Structure

  • PCA visualization: VAE latent samples are projected to RGB using principal component analysis (PCA)
  • Adaptive refinement: REPA-E adapts differently to each VAE's unique latent space characteristics
    • SD-VAE enhancement: Reduces high-frequency noise for smoother latent space
    • IN-VAE & VA-VAE enhancement: Add structural details to over-smoothed latent representations

3. End-to-End Tuning Improves VAE Performance

  • Enhanced drop-in replacement: E2E-VAE can serve as a drop-in replacement for their original counterparts in standard LDM training
  • Speed up training: Significantly accelerates generation training, achieving gFID of 3.46 in just 80 epochs (vs. 4.29 with VA-VAE and 7.90 with SD-VAE)
  • State-of-the-art generation quality: Achieves a gFID of 1.90 (w/o CFG) and 1.29 (w/ CFG) when training with REPA for 800 epochs
